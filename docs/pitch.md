# OffGridOS — Investor Pitch

## The Problem

Europe is adding residential solar at record pace, but the planning and installation process is still done with spreadsheets, WhatsApp threads, and PDF quotes. A homeowner who wants to go off-grid — or significantly reduce grid dependence — faces a fragmented journey: find an installer, trust their calculations, approve a quote they can't verify, and hand over five figures without a shared source of truth.

Installers have the opposite problem. They juggle dozens of projects across ad-hoc tools, rebuild the same calculations for every client, and spend hours on proposals that may never convert. There is no shared workspace between installer and homeowner. Every project starts from zero.

---

## The Solution

**OffGridOS is the digital twin platform for residential energy systems.**

A homeowner configures their roof surfaces, consumption profile, and budget. The system calculates panel yield by month, sizes the battery bank against real discharge curves, evaluates every component for compatibility, and produces a live cost summary — updated instantly as configuration changes.

The same project can be shared with an installer, who refines the design, substitutes components from a verified catalog, and hands it back for approval. Or an installer creates the configuration and invites the homeowner to review it. Either direction works. The digital twin is the contract.

---

## Why Now

Three forces are converging:

**1. Demand is structural, not cyclical.** Energy prices in Europe have permanently repriced. Household energy independence has moved from niche to mainstream. The Netherlands alone installed over 3 GW of residential PV in 2023. Germany, Belgium, France, and the Nordics are on similar trajectories.

**2. Installers are overwhelmed.** The bottleneck is not panels or batteries — it is qualified installers with the capacity to plan and quote. Tools that compress the planning cycle from days to hours are immediately valuable. An installer handling 50 projects a year with OffGridOS instead of 20 is a compelling ROI story.

**3. The equipment side is fragmenting.** Dozens of new battery brands, panel manufacturers, and inverter vendors are competing for shelf space. Installers need a curated, up-to-date catalog with real specs and pricing. That catalog, embedded in a workflow tool, is where distribution power accrues.

---

## The Market

**Addressable market:** ~4 million residential solar installations per year across Western Europe, growing at 25% CAGR. Average system value €15,000–€30,000. Installer planning and proposal costs represent 5–8% of system value — a €3–5B annual market in friction alone.

**Initial wedge:** independent installers and small installation firms (2–20 employees) in the Netherlands, Belgium, and Germany. These firms are too small for enterprise software and underserved by generic tools. There are approximately 8,000 such firms in the DACH+Benelux region.

---

## Business Model

**Three revenue streams, sequenced:**

**1. Installer SaaS (near-term)**
Installers pay a monthly subscription to manage unlimited projects, access the full component catalog, and use the proposal and sharing workflow. Target: €79–€149/month per installer seat. A firm doing 40 installations a year generates ~€15,000 in software value; the ask is modest.

**2. Equipment catalog listings (medium-term)**
Manufacturers and distributors pay to maintain verified catalog entries — specs, pricing, availability — directly in the tool their installers use. This is not advertising; it is the operational infrastructure of the supply chain. Pricing: €500–€2,000/month per brand per market.

**3. Homeowner marketplace (longer-term)**
Homeowners who configure their system can request quotes from verified installers in their region. Installers pay a lead fee or a small percentage of closed contracts. This creates the network effect: more homeowner configurations attract more installers, which improves quote quality for homeowners.

## Traction

OffGridOS is functional today. The core digital twin covers panel yield estimation, MPPT and battery compatibility evaluation, cost summary, and consumption planning. The component catalog includes major brands across panels, MPPTs, batteries, inverters, and enclosures. The app is in active development with real project data.

The product is pre-revenue and pre-scale — but the hard technical problem (accurate, real-time system modeling) is solved. What remains is distribution and the platform layer: multi-tenancy, sharing, and catalog monetization.

## Why This Becomes a Platform

Every project completed on OffGridOS generates structured data: what components were chosen, what configuration performed best, what the system cost, and how the homeowner's consumption profile looked. Aggregated, this is the most detailed dataset on residential off-grid system performance in Europe.

That dataset is the moat. It improves yield estimates, surfaces better component choices, and eventually enables OffGridOS to recommend configurations — not just model them. The tool becomes the standard. The standard becomes the marketplace. The marketplace becomes the infrastructure layer of the European residential energy transition.
